Step-By-Step Guide to Appealing Your Ticket

You have the right to contest the issuance of a parking, compliance, automated traffic law enforcement (i.e. red light) or automated speed camera enforcement violation. The timeline of the appeal process is as follows:

In Person:

  • You have seven (7) days from the date on the ticket to contest.
  • If you do not pay on contest within seven (7) days, a second Notice of Violation is mailed allowing twenty-one (21) days to request a hearing. (28 days from ticket date)
  • You will then have twenty-one (21) days to contest the issuance of an automated traffic law enforcement (i.e. red light) or automated speed camera enforcement violation. (28 days from ticket date)
  • If there is no response after twenty-one (21) days, you will be found liable by default. You may petition to set aside this default judgment if you appear in person within twenty-one (21) days. (69 days from ticket date)

Mail-In:

  • Mail a statement signed by the registered owner to the address below. Use the pre-addressed envelope provided with the ticket issued or the envelope provided with the mailed Notice of Violation. On the envelope, fill in the bubble noting Contest By Mail. If you Contest By Mail using the envelope provided with a Notice of Violation, include the stub at the end of the notice and fill in the bubble on the stub noting Contest By Mail.
  • In your statement: Set forth facts that establish a defense. See the Appeal tips and template provided on this website.
  • Mail to: City of Chicago, Department of Finance, P.O. Box 88292, Chicago, IL 60680-1292
  • If you are unsatisfied with your decision, you may appeal before a judge under Administrative Review in the Circuit Court of Cook County located at the Daley Center, 50 W. Washington St., Room 602. You must do this within 35 days of the Administrative Law Officer's decision.
